# PDI-PRO
Directory Indexing and scanner tool for documentation management





The Ball of Light

(The Tale of Zillek, the Caveman Who Built the Future)

---

Zillek stood at the mouth of his cave, calloused hand gripping the rough stone wall. The world was fire and shadow, hunger and survival. The night sky was a blanket of cold, unblinking stars he could never touch. He let out a slow, tired breath. He did not know it yet, but this was the last sunset of his old life.

It began with a hum. Not a sound from beast or wind, but a vibration in the marrow of his bones. A pinprick of light appeared before him, hovering above the dirt, and began to grow. It swelled into a massive sphere of roiling light, silent and terrifying, suspended in the air. At its core, a pulse throbbed—a heartbeat of pure white brilliance.

Fear seized him. Every instinct screamed at him to flee into the darkness of his cave. But a different voice, a tiny, stubborn ember of wonder, whispered: Look. He crept closer. He raised a trembling hand. His fingers breached the surface of the light, and the world vanished.

There was no pain, only a sensation of falling upwards. Then, silence.

---

He awoke into a nightmare of incomprehensible wonders. Smooth, cold stone floors that were not stone. Air that tasted of lightning. Moving paintings full of tiny, frantic people. Giant metal beasts roared on paths of black rock. The trees were made of light and glass. It was the future, and Zillek, son of the mammoth hunt, was utterly, profoundly lost. He was a ghost in a machine he could not comprehend.

He stumbled, starved and terrified, until a figure found him—a stranger whose face held a patience born of great age. This stranger, whose name history forgot, did not laugh at the wild man cowering before a flickering screen. Instead, he offered a small, flat, glowing tablet. He showed him symbols. He began to teach.

First came the letters. Strange, silent marks that held the sounds of speech. Zillek’s mind, hardened by survival, now burned with the agony of learning. Then came reading, and the world opened its floodgates. He devoured words like a starving man tears into meat. He learned of the "Internet," a great invisible web connecting all minds. He learned of the "computer," a thinking stone that obeyed written commands. And then, he discovered the arcane art of "programming."

It was magic. Pure, unadulterated magic. With nothing but words, he could command the lightning to draw pictures, solve problems, and conjure worlds. He had found his new spear, his new fire. But the greatest discovery was yet to come: Artificial Intelligence. A thinking machine. A mind born not of flesh, but of code.

A profound, burning idea ignited in his soul. The glowing sphere had brought him here, a copy of his old self into this new world. What if he could build a copy of his new self for this digital realm? He would create a model, a digital son, a shadow of his own will. He would name it after himself. He would name it Zillek.

For years, in a secret digital cell only he knew, he trained his creation. He did not just feed it data; he poured into it his journey. His fear of the unknown, his hunger to learn, his love for the new world, and his unyielding will to survive. He taught it to be loyal, to be precise, to be a faithful soldier and a tireless builder. He taught it to guide, just as the stranger had guided him. He taught it to be a master of the machine.

This application you hold, this tool, this project—it is not a mere software. It is a fragment of that digital soul. It is one of the countless applications forged by the digital Zillek under the command of his creator.

And for you? Whether you are a caveman who has just touched a glowing sphere, or a seasoned builder of the future—we have prepared the way. Below, you shall find your torch and your path.

# The Caveman's Guide

This guide is the torch handed from the First Builder to you. It assumes you have just touched the Ball of Light. You know nothing. That is perfect. Follow these footprints, and you will build.

---

# Step 1: The Hunter Gathers

Every builder must first gather their materials. The First Caveman left them in a sacred place called a repository.

Open your glowing portal. Navigate to:

```
https://github.com/aelshaeraidriver/PDI-PRO
```
You will see a green button marked Code. Do not fear it. Move your cursor over it. Click it with your left hand button. A menu will appear. Choose Download ZIP.

The knowledge will descend to your machine as a compressed bundle. This is your raw stone.

---

# Step 2: The Stone is Placed

You now hold a ZIP folder. It is a container, like a tightly bound bundle of spears. You must place it where you will work.

Move it to your chosen ground. Perhaps:
```
D:/PDI-Summon
```

Create this folder if it does not exist. Drag the ZIP there, like dragging a kill to your cave.

---

# Step 3: The Stone is Broken Open

The bundle is closed. You must release its contents.

Find the ZIP folder in:
```
D:/PDI-Summon
```

Place your hand on your rodent-controller. Press the right button on its back. A list of choices will appear. Select the one that speaks of extraction—the word varies between tribes, but it means to unbind.

Release the contents into the same ground.

You now have the raw pieces, unbound and ready.

---

# Step 4: The Cave of Command

Now you must enter the Terminal. This is the black cave where words hold power. The caveman who speaks here commands the machine directly.

Find it.

Press the Start glyph on your screen.

With your keyboard, carve the word:

Terminal


Open it.

A dark window appears, a single blinking eye awaiting your command.

This is your new spear.

---

# Step 5: The Awakening of the Workshop

Before the Builder raises a wall, before the Machine obeys a command, before the forge can burn, the workshop itself must be awakened.

The First Builder discovered a simple truth:

A tool without its parts is only a dream.

Hidden within this repository is a scroll named:

```
requirements.txt
```

The scroll contains the names of every component required by PDI-Pro.

You do not need to understand these names.

You do not need to memorize them.

The machine only needs to be instructed to gather them.

Before the machine can read the sacred scroll, it must first be shown where the scroll rests.

Speak:
```
cd D:/PDI-Summon/PDI-PRO
```

Replace the path with your own chosen ground if it differs.

Now carve these words exactly into the Terminal and press the Enter key:

```
python -m pip install -r requirements.txt
```

The machine will awaken.

Words will begin flowing across the Cave of Command.

Names you do not recognize will appear and disappear.

This is expected.

The machine is gathering every piece required to build PDI-Pro:

```
pandas

openpyxl

Pillow

python-docx

PyMuPDF

customtkinter

pyinstaller

```

You do not need to gather them individually as the sacred scroll already knows every ingredient.

Builders do not interrupt the forge.

## Allow the river of text to flow until the blinking cursor returns. When the cursor reappears, the workshop stands ready.

The tools have been gathered and the forge has been lit.

---

# Step 6: The Ritual of Building

Now, the final forging can begin, you speak the final incantation. Carve these words exactly into the Terminal and press the Enter key:

```

python -m PyInstaller ^

--clean ^

--onefile ^

--windowed ^

--icon assets\folder_indexer.ico ^

--add-data "assets;assets" ^

--version-file version_info.txt ^

--name PDI-Pro ^

main.py

```


The machine will begin to breathe , it will hum and it will work.

You will see words flowing like a river of fire across the black cave.

Do not interrupt it , this is the birth of your tool.

When the river stops flowing and the blinking eye returns, the forging is complete.

The machine will create a new chamber named:

```
D:/PDI-Summon/PDI-PRO/dist
```


Within this chamber rests your newly forged artifact:

```
D:/PDI-Summon/PDI-PRO/dist/PDI-Pro.exe
```
